History & Culture of Oman

Discover the rich history, vibrant traditions, and timeless cultural heritage of Oman.

Significant Historical Events

The Age of Maritime Trade

Oman’s history as a maritime power flourished during the 17th century, with its strategic location linking Asia, Africa, and Europe.

The Al Bu Said Dynasty

Established in 1744, the Al Bu Said dynasty modernized Oman and has ruled the nation ever since.

Traditional Customs

Traditional Dress

Men wear the dishdasha, a long robe, while women’s attire features colorful dresses with intricate embroidery, reflecting Oman’s cultural pride.

Oud Music

The Oud, a traditional stringed instrument, is central to Omani music, echoing the nation's artistic and soulful heritage.

Cultural Heritage

Frankincense Trail

The UNESCO-listed Frankincense Trail highlights Oman’s ancient role as a center of frankincense trade, with sites in Salalah and Dhofar.

Omani Hospitality

Omanis are known for their warm hospitality, offering dates and kahwa (Omani coffee) to visitors as a gesture of welcome.
